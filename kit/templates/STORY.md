# STORY

**The biographical compile-target.** This is the document that makes Principle #1 — *the code is the story* — operative. Every product behavior should be traceable back to something written here. If a behavior cannot be traced back, it does not belong in the product. If something is written here that the product does not honor, the product is incomplete.

This is not a marketing bio. It is not the LinkedIn version. It is the version where the formative crises are named with enough specificity that an agent reading them can recognize when a user is describing a similar one and respond accordingly. Vague answers produce vague products. The Method assumes a builder who has lived something specific enough to compile.

Fill each field below substantively. The coverage scorer will tell you when each field is sufficient.

## Origin

Where you came from. Family, geography, formative early environment. The conditions that shaped how you read the world before you had words for it.

## Formative crisis

The events that broke you, that you survived, or that broke something you cared about. The places your refusal list comes from — the things you will never let your product do, because you have seen what happens when those things happen.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="formative_crisis" required="true" min_words="150" -->
[Describe the crises that shaped you. Not every hardship — the load-bearing ones. The ones that make you flinch when you see the pattern recurring in someone else. Be specific enough that the agent reading this could recognize the pattern when a user is describing a version of it. If the crisis is ongoing or unresolved, say so.]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## Competence pattern

What you have done well, and the discipline that made you good at it. Not a resume — the underlying capability the resume happens to be evidence of. The Method assumes the builder is encoding *real* competence into the product, not borrowed authority.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="competence_pattern" required="true" min_words="100" -->
[Describe the competence you actually have. Where did it come from — training, repetition, mentorship, hard environments? What specifically can you do that most people in your domain cannot? What does the discipline behind that competence look like in practice — daily, weekly, under pressure?]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## Failure pattern

Where you have failed, and what the failures taught you. The Method assumes the builder has a calibrated relationship with their own limits. A builder who has not failed cannot encode honest fallibility into the product. A builder who refuses to name their failures will build a product that hides its own.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="failure_pattern" required="true" min_words="100" -->
[Describe failures you can name. What you tried, what broke, what you took too long to admit, what you finally accepted. What the failure cost — money, relationships, reputation, time, health. What the failure changed about how you make decisions now.]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## Recovery arc (if applicable)

If you have rebuilt from something — addiction, illness, bankruptcy, divorce, exile, trauma — describe the rebuild. The recovery arc is often the engine of the product, because the user the product serves is often a version of the builder mid-recovery.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="recovery_arc" required="false" min_words="80" -->
[Describe the rebuild, if there is one. What collapsed, what the bottom looked like, what you stood up first, who helped, what is still in progress. If this field does not apply to you, mark it N/A and explain briefly why your story does not include a recovery arc.]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## Present purpose

Why you are building now. What you are trying to compile into product. The Method assumes the builder has a non-trivial answer to "why this, why now, why you" — and that the answer is consistent with the rest of this document.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="present_purpose" required="true" min_words="100" -->
[Describe what you are building and why now. What changed for you that made building this the right next thing. What would be lost if you did not build it. What you specifically can encode into it that no one else can — not because you are special, but because the bio above gives you specific compile-time inputs no one else has.]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## The users

Who specifically you are building for, and what about your story makes you uniquely able to serve them. The Method assumes the user is real — a population the builder can describe with specificity, not a marketing persona.

<!-- KIT:FIELD name="the_users" required="true" min_words="120" -->
[Describe the users. Who they are, how they got there, what they are dealing with that the product is meant to help with. What about your story makes you the right person to build for them. What patterns you recognize in them that another builder would miss. What they need the product to never do, because of where they are coming from.]
<!-- KIT:END -->

---

## Trace

Every behavior in your product should trace back to one of the fields above. If a feature is proposed and you cannot point to a field of STORY.md that justifies it, the feature does not belong in the product. The trace IS the audit — Principle #1 made operative.
